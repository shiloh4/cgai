Final Project: Dream Worlds

Welcome to the CGAI final project! In this project, you are tasked with applying the techniques learned in this class to develop a dreamed world with fields and particles. We have designed two tracks for the final project: the Creative Expression Track and the Technical Exploration Track, which differ in their emphasis on producing visually appealing renderings and investigating in-depth technical implementations, respectively. You may choose one of the two tracks by focusing on artistic creation or technical exploration. This is an open-ended project that allows you to collaborate with teammates and explore topics related to the four core themes studied during the semester: SDF, NeRF, GS, and Physics.

Requirements

Creative Expression Track

For the creative expression track, you are expected to generate one rendered image that demonstrates both complexity and visual appeal. You are expected to integrate elements such as implicit surfaces, rays, volumes, Gaussian splats, and physics to construct a rendering scene that artistically represents the chosen theme and stands out for its aesthetic quality. We recommend that you start from one of the GLSL assignments (e.g., A1A, A2A, A3, or A4) and enhance it to fulfill your rendering goal. You may also choose to create your own starter code for this project.


The requirements for Creative Expression Track include:

  • (1) The final rendered scene must be sufficiently complex and visually appealing.
  • (2) The final rendered scene must include at least one of the following elements: SDF, radiance field, splats, or physics.

Technical Exploration Track

For the technical exploration track, you are expected to investigate one technical topic related to this class in depth. For example, you may explore the SDF representation of an arbitrary shape, a ray tracing algorithm for a particular type of participating media such as smoke or fire, a point-based rendering method, or a physics simulation method for solids or fluids. You may also expand one of our previous programming assignments to achieve improved results. For instance, you may attempt to scale up the number of particles in our current Gaussian splats implementation or increase the depth of neural networks in the NeRF homework to achieve better rendering outcomes. The final results must be reported rigorously in a technical report. You must summarize the motivation, methodology, experimental data, and key findings in the report to support your conclusions.


The requirements for Technical Exploration Track include:

  • (1) By default, the exploration topic must be related to one of the four central topics studied in the class: SDF, radiance field, splats, or physics. Please discuss your plan with the instructor if you wish to explore a topic outside this scope.
  • (2) You are expected to submit a detailed technical report (3–4 pages) documenting the full process of your exploration, including motivation, methodology, experiments, and conclusions.

Starter Code

You may begin your project based on any of the previous assignments.

Teamwork

This project allows teamwork. Each team may consist of up to three members. Please discuss your plan with the instructor if you wish to include more than three members in your team.

Submission

Creative Expression Track

  • Submit your final image and/or final video.
  • Submit a short description of your work (1-2 pages). The description should include the following contents:
    1. - Image Overview: Provide a one-paragraph summary of the expressive theme and the techniques you used to realize it.
    2. - Implementation: Provide a one-paragraph summary of the key implementation aspects.
    3. - Contributions: If this is a team project, clearly describe each team member’s contributions.
  • Submit your source code.

Technical Exploration Track

  • Submit a technical report (3–4 pages, single-column format) written in the style of a short scientific paper. The report must include the following sections:
    1. - Problem: Briefly summarize the question to explore, and clearly state the motivation, goals, and main contributions.
    2. - Method: Describe the mathematical models, algorithms, and key implementation details. Include relevant equations, diagrams, and pseudocode structure as needed. Justify major design choices.
    3. - Results: Present qualitative and/or quantitative results. Include visual outputs or plots and describe key observations.
    4. - Conclusion: Summarize key findings. If this is a team project, specify each member’s contribution. Optionally, mention limitations or future directions.
  • Submit your source code.

Grading

Creative Expression Track

The grading policy for the total 16 points of the final project is specified as below.

  • Final image or animation (4 pts)
    1. - We will assess the visual quality and correctness of the generated graphics, including coherence, technical execution, and relation to the chosen theme.
  • Technical Implementation (8 pts)
    1. - We will assess the correctness, complexity, and completeness of the implemented pipeline, including model choices, algorithmic components, and integration.
  • Creative Expression (4 pts)
    1. - We will evaluate how effectively the submission conveys the intended expressive theme through visuals, with respect to originality, aesthetic quality, and coherence.
  • Extra credits (up to 2 pts) will be given for technical innovations or visual quality clearly beyond expectations.

Technical Exploration Track

The grading policy for the total 16 points of the final project is specified as below.

  • Technical report (4 pts)
    1. - We will assess the clarity, structure, and completeness of the report, including problem motivation, method details, and result interpretation.
  • Technical Implementation (8 pts)
    1. - We will evaluate correctness and efficiency of the implementation, including mathematical formulation, algorithm design, and implementationquality.
  • Results and Analysis (4 pts)
    1. - We will assess the quality and rigor of the results, including visualizations, performance, and observations derived from experiments.
  • Extra credits (up to 2 pts) will be given for technically ambitious contributions with clearly novel algorithms or in-depth analysis.
